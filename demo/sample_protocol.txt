INSTITUTIONAL REVIEW BOARD PROTOCOL SUBMISSION
==============================================

Protocol Number: PEDI-2026-0047
Version: 1.0 | Date: May 15, 2026

Study Title: A Phase II, Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Study
to Evaluate the Efficacy and Safety of MetaGlyX-400 in Pediatric Patients
with Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus

Sponsor: NovaCure Therapeutics, Inc.
Principal Investigator: Dr. Sarah Chen, MD, PhD
  Department of Pediatric Endocrinology
  University Medical Center

1. STUDY OVERVIEW
-----------------
This Phase II randomized controlled trial evaluates MetaGlyX-400, a novel
GLP-1/GIP dual receptor agonist, in pediatric patients aged 8-16 years with
Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM). The study will enroll approximately 120
participants across 8 clinical sites.

Duration: 52 weeks (12-week treatment + 40-week follow-up)
Design: Double-blind, placebo-controlled, 2:1 randomization

2. STUDY POPULATION
-------------------
Inclusion Criteria:
- Age 8-16 years at screening
- Diagnosed with T2DM per ADA criteria
- HbA1c 7.0-11.0% at screening
- BMI >= 85th percentile for age and sex

Exclusion Criteria:
- Type 1 diabetes or MODY
- eGFR < 60 mL/min/1.73m2
- Active hepatic disease (ALT > 3x ULN)
- Pregnancy or lactation

3. STUDY PROCEDURES
-------------------
Visit Schedule:
- Screening (Week -2)
- Baseline/Randomization (Week 0)
- Treatment visits (Weeks 2, 4, 8, 12)
- Follow-up visits (Weeks 16, 24, 36, 52)

Primary Endpoint: Change in HbA1c from baseline to Week 12
Secondary Endpoints: Fasting plasma glucose, body weight, BMI z-score

4. RISK ASSESSMENT
------------------
Known Risks of MetaGlyX-400:
- Gastrointestinal: Nausea (32%), vomiting (18%), diarrhea (12%)
- Injection site reactions (8%)
- Hypoglycemia when combined with insulin (5%)
- HEPATIC: Elevated liver enzymes requiring monitoring every 4 weeks
  with protocol-mandated discontinuation if ALT > 5x ULN
  ** LONG-TERM HEPATIC MONITORING REQUIRED FOR 24 MONTHS POST-TREATMENT **

Risk Classification: GREATER THAN MINIMAL RISK
Regulatory Basis: 45 CFR 46.405 — Research involving greater than minimal
risk but presenting the prospect of direct benefit to individual subjects.

5. INFORMED CONSENT
-------------------
5.1 Parental/Guardian Consent
Written informed consent will be obtained from at least one parent or
legally authorized representative before any study procedures. The consent
form describes study purpose, procedures, risks (GI effects, injection
site reactions, hypoglycemia), alternatives, and voluntary participation.

5.2 Participant Assent
For participants aged 8-11 years: A simplified verbal explanation of
the study will be provided using age-appropriate language. The child's
willingness to participate will be assessed and documented.

[NOTE: No written assent procedure is described for participants aged 12-16.
The protocol does not address assent for adolescents capable of providing
meaningful written assent.]

[NOTE: The consent form does NOT disclose the long-term hepatic monitoring
requirement described in Section 4. The consent form only mentions GI
effects, injection site reactions, and hypoglycemia as risks.]

6. DATA MANAGEMENT
------------------
6.1 De-identification
All participant data will be coded using a unique alphanumeric identifier.
No direct identifiers (name, DOB, SSN, MRN) will be stored in the study
database. A crosswalk file linking codes to identifiers will be maintained
in a separate, access-controlled system by the PI.

6.2 Data Storage
Electronic data will be stored on encrypted, HIPAA-compliant servers
maintained by the University Medical Center IT department. Paper source
documents will be stored in locked cabinets in the PI's office.

Data retention period: 15 years from study completion, per FDA 21 CFR 11.

6.3 Data Sharing
Coded study data will be shared with BioSync Research, LLC (Contract
Research Organization) for statistical analysis and monitoring. Data
sharing is governed by a confidentiality agreement between the University
and BioSync Research.

[NOTE: The data sharing arrangement with BioSync Research is covered only
by a confidentiality agreement, NOT a Business Associate Agreement (BAA)
as required by HIPAA when sharing Protected Health Information.]

7. SAFETY MONITORING
--------------------
A Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) will review safety data quarterly.
Serious Adverse Events (SAEs) will be reported to the IRB within 7 days.
Non-serious adverse events will be reported within 15 days.
