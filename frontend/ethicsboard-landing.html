The Problem

Ethics review
is a coordination
failure.

Every hospital, university, and pharmaceutical company must submit research involving human subjects to an Institutional Review Board (IRB) before any study can begin. The board reviews ethics, consent, risk, and data privacy — then a qualified human chair must sign off.

Today this process runs on email chains. A policy reviewer finishes their work and sends it to the legal team, who send it to risk, who send it to the chair — with no structured handoff, no traceability, and no coordination layer. A single missing clause in a consent form restarts the clock.

Patient studies get delayed. Cures get delayed. And there is no audit trail that regulators can rely on.

The Demo

Three deficiencies.
Planted deliberately.

The demo protocol contains real IRB deficiency categories. If you show this document to an actual IRB professional, they will recognize the issues immediately.

Deficiency 01
Ethics Agent
Missing Written Assent for Ages 12–16
The protocol describes verbal explanation only for ages 8–11 and makes no provision for written assent from older participants. Minors capable of assent are legally required to provide it in writing.
45 CFR 46.408 — Common Rule
Deficiency 02
Ethics Agent
Hepatic Monitoring Not Disclosed in Consent
The risk assessment table (Section 4) mentions long-term hepatic monitoring as a safety requirement. The informed consent documents make no mention of this, creating a material disclosure gap.
ICH E6(R2) 4.8.10 — Good Clinical Practice
Deficiency 03
Privacy Agent
No Business Associate Agreement for CRO
Data sharing with BioSync Research (the appointed CRO) is governed by a "confidentiality agreement." Any entity receiving protected health information must have an executed Business Associate Agreement — not a standard NDA.
HIPAA 45 CFR 164.308(b)(1)
